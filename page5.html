MOLTING
From time to time hermit crabs must shed its exoskeleton in order to grow. Hermit crabs have the ability to grow back their body parts. They may stop moving for a few weeks or even a few months.  Some hermit crab  owners may think that their hermit crab is dying or dead while they are molting. If your hermit crab does not smell it is probably molting, it is not dead. It's pretty hard to predict when your hermit crab is going to molt, if you have  not had a hermit crab before.

Hermit crabs will be moving more than usual. Their skin will become a different color. A lighter color - like an ashy color. Since they lose 90% of their calcium when they shed their  exoskeleton, they will eat their old exoskeleton to regain it.
